*Bond with a Primal Beast*

A Beast Master forms a mystical bond with a special animal, drawing on primal magic and a deep connection to the natural world.

## Subclass Features

| Level | Subclass Features                 |
| :---: | :-------------------------------- |
|   3   | Primal Companion, Beast Master Spells |
|   7   | Exceptional Training, Primal Steed               |
|  11  | Bestial Fury                    |
|  15   | Shared Spells                |


### Level 3: Primal Companion [none]

You magically summon a primal beast, which draws strength from your bond with nature.  
Choose its stat block: **Beast of the Land**, **Beast of the Sea**, or **Beast of the Sky**. You determine the kind of animal it is, choosing a form appropriate to the stat block. Whatever form you choose, it bears subtle primal markings that indicate its supernatural origin.  

The beast is Friendly to you and your allies and obeys your commands. It vanishes if you die.

#### Initiative and Combat [none]

The beast shares your Initiative count and acts on your turn. You determine the order in which you and your beast take your actions during that turn.  

The beast can move and use its Reaction normally. However, it takes only the Dodge action on its turn unless you command it otherwise.

You can command the beast in one of the following ways:

- As a Bonus Action, you command it to take an action from its stat block or another action available to it.  
- When you take the Attack action, you can replace one of your attacks to command the beast to take the **Beast’s Strike** action.

If you have the Incapacitated condition, the beast acts independently and is no longer limited to the Dodge action.

#### Bestial Bond [none]

Your supernatural bond allows you to operate through your companion as an extension of your own will.

While your Primal Companion is within 100 feet of you:

- You can communicate with it telepathically.  
- As an action, you can perceive through the beast’s senses until the start of your next turn. During this time, you are blinded and deafened with regard to your own senses.  
- When you cast a spell with a range of Touch, the beast can deliver the spell as if it had cast it. Doing so requires the beast to use its Reaction.

#### Restoring or Replacing the Beast [none]

If the beast has died within the last hour, you can take a Magic action to touch it and expend a spell slot. The beast returns to life after 1 minute with all its Hit Points restored.  

Whenever you finish a Long Rest, you can summon a different primal beast in an unoccupied space within 5 feet of you. You choose its stat block and appearance. If you already have a beast from this feature, the old one vanishes when the new one appears.


### Level 3: Beast Master Spells [none]

Your bond grants you access to additional spells. These spells are always prepared for you and do not count against the number of Ranger spells you prepare.

| Ranger Level | Spells               |
|--------------|----------------------|
| 3            | Animal Friendship    |
| 5            | Animal Messenger     |
| 9            | Conjure Animals      |
| 13           | Dominate Beast       |
| 17           | Commune with Nature  |


### Level 7: Exceptional Training [none]

Your companion’s discipline improves.

When you use a Bonus Action to command your Primal Companion to take an action, you can also command it to take the Dash, Disengage, Dodge, or Help action using its Bonus Action.  

In addition, whenever the beast hits with an attack roll, it can deal your choice of Force damage or its normal damage type.


### Level 7: Primal Steed [none]

Your bond deepens, allowing your companion to manifest as a true primal war-mount.

Whenever you summon your Primal Companion, you can choose for it to appear in its Steed Form.  

At 7th level, only a **Beast of the Land** or a **Beast of the Sea** can assume Steed Form.  
Starting at 11th level, a **Beast of the Sky** can also assume this form.

While in Steed Form:

- The beast’s size increases by one category (to a maximum of Large).  
- It gains additional maximum Hit Points equal to twice your Ranger level.  
- Its weapon attacks deal an extra 1d4 damage.  
- The beast is considered a suitable mount for you.

The Steed Form lasts until the beast is reduced to 0 Hit Points or dismissed.


### Level 11: Bestial Fury [none]
Your companion’s ferocity grows alongside your own.

When you command your Primal Companion to take the **Beast’s Strike** action, the beast can make two Beast’s Strike attacks instead of one.  

In addition, the first time each turn the beast hits a creature affected by your **Hunter’s Mark**, it deals extra Force damage equal to the bonus damage of that spell.


### Level 15: Shared Spells [none]
Your magic and your companion’s essence are fully intertwined.

When you cast a spell targeting yourself, you can also affect your Primal Companion with the spell if the beast is within 30 feet of you.
