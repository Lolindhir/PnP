## Half-Race Characters [Half-Races]

You can create a character that is a combination of two different races, representing a mixed heritage.

### Appearance and Lifespan [none]

When you create a Half-Race character, you can freely determine its physical appearance by combining characteristics of both parent races. This includes features such as size, build, coloration, and other distinguishing characteristics.
You also choose which race determines your character’s lifespan, or you may define an appropriate lifespan based on both ancestries.


### Traits [none]

Each race provides a number of traits, which are divided into two categories:

- **Minor Traits**
- **Major Traits**

When creating a Half-Race character, determine your available traits as follows:

- Add together the total number of Minor Traits from both parent races, then halve the result (rounding up).  
- Add together the total number of Major Traits from both parent races, subtract 1 from the total, then halve the result (rounding up).

You can then choose a number of Minor and Major Traits up to these limits. These traits can be selected freely from either of the two parent races and can be combined in any way.

### Restrictions [none]

- You can select each trait only once unless a trait explicitly states otherwise.  
- If two traits would overlap or conflict, the DM determines how they function together or whether one must be chosen instead.  
- The DM has final say on any unusual combinations or edge cases.
